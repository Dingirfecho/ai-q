The SAMA Framework

"A method for the change AI demands."

95% of organizations investing in AI fail to capture the expected value. The obstacle is not technological: it is organizational. SAMA is a Change Management framework that integrates three disciplines typically treated separately —narrative, presence and cadence— on top of the AI-Q diagnostic.

The Context

Why a new framework

Traditional change management frameworks were designed for a different era. They assumed long cycles, discrete changes and a clear separation between technology and human behavior. AI breaks all three assumptions: the cycle is continuous, the change is systemic, and the technology embeds directly into everyone's daily work.

AI transformation demands action on three simultaneous fronts: the narrative front, the emotional front, and the operational front. Ignoring any of the three produces cosmetic transformations.

SAMA integrates these three fronts on top of the AI-Q diagnostic. It does not replace established methodologies: it updates them for an era where transformation is simultaneously technical, narrative, and emotional.
Layer 0 — Foundation

The AI-Q diagnostic

Before activating any change lever, the organization must know where it stands. The AI-Q provides that diagnostic across four dimensions that combine into 16 organizational types.

The diagnostic produces two essential outputs: identification of the current type and selection of the target type. The gap between them defines what each sprint must work on.

Layer 1 — The three activators

The pillars of change

Each pillar addresses a distinct failure mode in AI transformations. The three operate simultaneously and none works in isolation.

Pillar 1
Storytelling
Defeats fear

People are not mobilized by dashboards; they are mobilized by stories. Storytelling in SAMA is a strategic discipline that builds three articulated narratives.

Executive Narrative
A three-act story senior leadership must be able to tell in five minutes: where we come from, the inflection point, and where we are going.
Internal Hero Arc
Every employee must answer yes to one question: what can I do with AI that I couldn't before? If unclear, that person will not join the change.
Library of Living Cases
Short testimonials from colleagues down the hall, not foreign case studies. One new story every two weeks across all internal channels.
Pillar 2
Mindfulness
Sustains the journey

Every transformation crosses a valley where the old no longer works and the new does not yet perform. Mindfulness is the organizational capacity to sustain attention, energy, and listening through that valley.

Full Attention in Decisions
Committee meetings that open with two minutes of centering and close with one minute of reflection. It changes decision quality.
Energy Management
Sprint and recovery cycles. After every change sprint, one week of consolidation without new initiatives.
Listening to Resistance
Resistance is not an obstacle: it is information. Formal spaces where skeptics can voice concerns without being labeled.
Pillar 3
Agile
Accelerates progress

Not the software development methodology applied to organizational change: a cadence principle. Transformation is built through short cycles, visible wins, and continuous adjustments.

Six-Week Sprints
Each sprint has specific focus: one AI-Q dimension, a bounded set of initiatives, clear metrics. Nothing moves all at once.
Prioritized Backlog
Every initiative has a business owner —not a tech owner—, a measurable success metric, and connection to an AI-Q dimension.
Retros and Biweekly Demos
What worked, what didn't, what we adjust. Demos produce social evidence that transformation is actually happening.
Layer 2 — Operating engine

The Sense · Align · Move · Anchor cycle

The three pillars integrate into a six-week cycle that repeats until the AI-Q gap is closed. Each phase activates a dominant pillar.

Week 1
Sense
Listen to the organization. Identify pain, traction, and resistance.
Week 2
Align
Calibrate the narrative and sprint objectives with what was heard.
Weeks 3 to 5
Move
Execute sprint initiatives with agile cadence.
Week 6
Anchor
Consolidate advances in structure, process, and culture.
The Anchor phase is critical. It is where advances become institutionalized: job descriptions get updated, operating processes get modified, measurement systems get adjusted. Without Anchor, every sprint demands the same heroic effort as the previous one.
Measurement system

Metrics by pillar

A framework without metrics is a statement of intent. SAMA measures progress and outcome in each component, through a single dashboard accessible across the organization.

Pillar Progress Indicator Outcome Indicator
AI-Q Score by dimension in quarterly assessment Movement from current type toward target
Storytelling Reach and frequency of narratives deployed Comprehension and adherence to the story
Mindfulness Listening spaces held and participation rate Organizational energy index and turnover
Agile Sprint velocity and completion rate Initiatives in production with measurable value
Critical conditions

Without these, SAMA won't work

Four conditions without which the framework cannot deliver results. These are not recommendations: they are requirements.

01
Active Executive Sponsorship
Board approval is not enough. The CEO and at least two members of the executive committee must personally engage in demos and retrospectives.
02
Real Resource Commitment
The transformation team cannot be an additional duty. It requires formally assigned dedication with its own performance metrics.
03
Tolerance for the Valley
The committee must commit in writing to not abandon the framework when indicators get worse before they get better.
04
Business Ownership
If the transformation is still seen as a technology matter, the Leadership dimension of AI-Q will never close.
