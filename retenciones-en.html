●SOLUTIONSHEET 08 / WITHHOLDING TAX
§ Solution · Accounting & treasury

Automated processor for withholding tax certificates/

"No typing, no delays, with built-in audit."

Turn every withholding certificate you receive — PDF, scan, or photo — into a SAP-ready row in your spreadsheet. An end-to-end solution designed specifically for Argentine accounting teams.

§01 · The problem

Days lost each month

If your team opens each certificate, identifies the agent, separates taxes line by line, looks up the SAP code, and loads it manually, you're losing 3 to 8 minutes per document. Multiplied by typical monthly volume, that's days of administrative work that can be recovered.

+ 3 to 8 min per document

And that's not counting typing errors, mis-assigned SAP codes, and time spent on accounting corrections.

§02 · The solution

How it works, in 5 steps

An end-to-end automation that receives certificates as they arrive — in any format — and delivers them processed, classified, and ready to post.

01
Drop in Drive
The certificate is dropped into a Google Drive folder, or arrives by email if you prefer that flow.
02
OCR reading
The document is digitized, even if it's a low-quality scan or a phone photo.
03
AI extraction
Identifies the withholding agent and splits each tax into independent lines.
04
SAP mapping
Each withholding is mapped to its exact ledger account — income tax, gross-receipts, or reclassification.
05
Sheet ready
One row per tax line in Sheets, with Argentine formatting and reconciliation fields.

§04 · What's included

A complete solution

◆
Per-row traceability
Every record loads with a confidence score (0–100), link to the original file, name, and timestamp. Auditing a record takes seconds, not minutes.
⚠
Safeguards
When the tool isn't sure, it says so — flags the record as "Review" or assigns it to a reclassification account, instead of guessing.
▲
Argentine rules embedded
Normalization to format NNNN-NNNNNNNN, rate corrections, and identification of the 24 IIBB jurisdictions with their aliases.
◇
Custom chart of accounts
The SAP dictionary is tailored to your internal nomenclature. Your tax ID and company name are protected by explicit rules — a common error in generic solutions.
