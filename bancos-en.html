The problem

Every bank, its own format

If your accounting firm, your SMB or your admin team spends hours opening PDFs and copying transactions by hand — or typing them straight into the accounting system — you already know the real cost. It's not just the time: it's the worn-out attention, the transcription errors, and the inability to close the month on time.

"PDFs aren't tables: they're text pulled from a visual layout." Pasting into Excel gives you a mess. The "Convert PDF to Excel" option works 60% of the time. The other 40% has to be cleaned up row by row.

What matters

Two details that make the difference

◆
One prompt per bank
We don't use a generic prompt. Each bank has its own with explicit rules: how to clean amounts (1.250,50 → 1250.50), date formatting, required JSON columns, what parts of the PDF to ignore. This is what separates a solution that works sometimes from one that works every month.
▲
Long PDFs get split
Statements of 30, 50+ pages exceed Gemini's token limit and accuracy drops. For those cases there's a "splitter" scenario that cuts the PDF into 5-page chunks, processes each separately, and consolidates everything into the same sheet.
◇
Argentine number formats
Period as thousand separator and comma as decimal (1.250,50) breaks any naive import. The prompt resolves this before returning the JSON: clean numbers, correct sign, ready for formulas.
⚙
Parallel processing
Drop 10 statements into Drive and all 10 get processed at the same time, in minutes. No human bottleneck. The queue is Make's problem.
★
Full traceability
Every execution is logged in Make: which file came in, what was extracted, when. If something doesn't add up, there's a log to audit. No black boxes.
▼
Importable to any ERP
Since the output is a spreadsheet, you can export it to CSV and feed it into Tango, Bejerman, Holistor, Xubio or whichever system you use. The import itself can also be automated if your ERP supports it.

Honest caveats

Limitations worth knowing

!
PDFs must be native, not scanned. If your bank sends a photo of a printout, you'll need to add an OCR step — more expensive and less precise. Most Argentine banks already issue native PDFs.
!
If the bank changes the layout, the prompt needs adjustment. Not frequent — banks change layouts every few years — but when it happens, an update is needed. This is part of ongoing maintenance.
!
It doesn't replace human review. The system is highly accurate (>99% in testing), but it's worth eyeballing the sheet before pushing it to production. That's the whole point: review at a glance, not rebuild from scratch.
$
It has running costs. Make, PDF.co and Gemini charge per use. For a typical SMB volume, that's USD 20–50/month in subscriptions, against several person-hours saved.
