CHAPTER · I

Foreword

A.I.·Q is to organizations what Myers-Briggs is to individuals: a frame for naming what you already feel but cannot articulate.

Every executive team I have worked with says some version of the same sentence: "We know we should be doing more with AI, but we don't know what kind of company we are when it comes to AI." This guide answers that question across four dimensions: Strategy, Adoption, Talent, and Integration — yielding sixteen distinct types.

Each type is neither good nor bad. Each is a snapshot of where you are now, and a map of where you can go next.

The Four Axes

Structure ⇌ Strategy

Whether your AI initiatives are scattered tactical bets or aligned to a clear strategic thesis.

Pioneer ⇌ Reactive

Whether you adopt AI proactively to gain advantage or defensively to keep pace.

Builder ⇌ Trainer

Whether your edge is in technology and tooling or in people and capability.

Integrated ⇌ Experimental

Whether AI lives in production workflows or stays trapped in pilots.

How to Use This Guide

  • Take the assessment at ai-q.fit/quiz — five minutes, twenty questions.
  • Read your type page; share the link with your leadership team.
  • Discuss the four axes with your team; place yourselves on each individually before reading the result.
  • Choose one phase from your type's "path forward" to begin in the next ninety days.
  • Reassess every six months — types are not fixed.
