§01 · The problem

The spreadsheet that doesn't work for you

If you run your gym with a spreadsheet — like most — you've already got 80% of the work done. What's missing is that this sheet stops being a dead file and starts telling you who hasn't paid, showing you how much each plan invoices, and sending follow-up emails without you writing them one by one.

"You don't know how much each plan generates, nor how many late payers you have this month."

And every student who lapses and isn't contacted again is revenue walking away for no reason.

§02 · The solution

Three automations, one spreadsheet

Three independent scenarios that share your current Google Sheet. Each one solves a concrete pain point and runs manually with one click or on a schedule (every Monday at 9am, for example).

01
Plans Report
Breakdown of every pass: price, enrolled students, projected revenue. You know in seconds which plan is your most profitable and how much you should be billing this month.
02
Payment Status
Cross-references students with payments and returns current, late, and amounts owed. Arrives in your inbox without you opening the sheet or filtering anything.
03
Reactivation
Identifies students who lapsed or paused, and sends them a reactivation email in the tone you define. The highest-ROI automation.

§05 · Architecture

A layer on top of your current sheet

This isn't a migration to a new system or an expensive sports-management tool. It's a layer of intelligence on top of your current sheet: the database doesn't change, the people using it don't either. The only thing that changes is that the sheet starts producing reports and communicating on its own.

Three independent Make.com scenarios read your Google Sheet, process the data, and trigger the actions. No servers to administer, no code to maintain, no lock-in: if tomorrow you want to modify an email or the report schedule, it's all visible and editable.
