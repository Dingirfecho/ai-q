CASE STUDIES · 2024

Introduction

Sixteen organizations. Sixteen types. Sixteen turning points.

The cases that follow are sanitized composites drawn from engagements between 2022 and 2024. Names, industries, and figures are altered; the patterns are real. Each is mapped to one of the A.I.·Q types so you can locate yourself by analogy.

A.I.·Q Cases

Case · CPBI · Financial Services

Strategic Leader · Banking · 12,000 employees

The Edge That Almost Eroded

A regional bank had three years of compounding AI advantage in fraud detection. By month thirty-six, the model was a generation behind newer entrants and the team was complacent.

Intervention: Quarterly disruption-scanning council, dedicated retention budget for senior modelers, and a forced-rotation policy that kept the team intellectually mobile.

A.I.·Q Cases

Case · SRTE · Manufacturing

Chaos Pilot · Industrial · 4,200 employees

Forty-One Tools, One Strategy

Audit revealed forty-one separate AI/ML tools across business units, with no shared inventory and overlapping vendor contracts.

Intervention: Ninety-day moratorium on new procurement, centralized inventory, sunset criteria, and a single VP-of-AI accountability line.

A.I.·Q Cases

Case · SPTE · Retail

Over-Engineer · DTC Apparel · 800 employees

The Recommendation Engine Nobody Asked For

An eight-person ML team shipped a sophisticated recommendation system in six months. Adoption: 3%. The merchandising team had a different mental model of "similar."

Intervention: Reframe the team's charter from build to diagnose. Embed an ML engineer with merchandising for one quarter before any new model is greenlit.
